My First Blog Post: Building This Portfolio

Published on March 11, 2026

Welcome to my new little corner of the internet! I'm excited to finally have a space to share my thoughts, projects, and whatever else comes to mind. For my very first post, it felt fitting to talk about the creation of this portfolio website itself.

This site was built using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, keeping things simple and lightweight while still aiming for a modern feel. I wanted to build something from scratch rather than rely on heavy frameworks, so I could fully understand how each part of the site works and have full control over the design.

The design process was surprisingly fun. From choosing the dark theme and purple accents to structuring the layout of the sections, I tried to keep everything clean, minimal, and easy to navigate.

More than anything, I wanted the site to feel intentional. Every section, from the hero banner to the project cards, was designed to balance functionality and simplicity, while still reflecting a bit of my personality.

While building the site, I also experimented with AI-assisted development using tools like Claude. It was interesting to use an AI assistant for brainstorming layout ideas, debugging small issues, and speeding up certain parts of the development process. At the same time, I wanted to make sure I understood the code I was writing, which is another reason I chose to keep the stack simple.

Ultimately, this portfolio is meant to be a space where I can share not only my projects, but also ideas I find interesting, whether that’s technology, mathematics, problem solving, or things I’m learning along the way.

This space will likely evolve over time as I work on new projects and begin my Master’s in Artificial Intelligence in Germany. For now, though, this first post marks the beginning.
